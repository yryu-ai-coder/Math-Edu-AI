📐 1. Congruent Figures

Two figures are congruent if they have the same shape and size.

🔍 Congruent Triangles

Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding sides and angles are equal.

Congruence Criteria:

  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): All three sides are equal
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): Two sides and the included angle are equal
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): Two angles and the included side are equal
  • AAS (Angle-Angle-Side): Two angles and a non-included side are equal

📏 2. Triangle Properties

Triangles have special properties that help us solve geometric problems.

📐 Triangle Sum Theorem

The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is always 180°.

∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

💡 Example: Finding Missing Angle

In triangle ABC, if ∠A = 60° and ∠B = 45°, find ∠C.

∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

60° + 45° + ∠C = 180°

105° + ∠C = 180°

∠C = 180° - 105° = 75°

🔗 3. Parallel Lines and Transversals

When a transversal crosses parallel lines, special angle relationships are created.

📐 Corresponding Angles

When a transversal crosses parallel lines, corresponding angles are equal.

💡 Example: Finding Angles

If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal and one angle is 120°, find the other angles.

Corresponding angle = 120°

Alternate interior angle = 120°

Vertical angle = 120°

Supplementary angle = 180° - 120° = 60°

📖 1. Pythagorean Theorem

In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides.

📐 Formula: a² + b² = c²

Where:

  • a, b = legs of the right triangle
  • c = hypotenuse (longest side, opposite to right angle)

Example: If a = 3 and b = 4, find c.

c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25

c = √25 = 5

✏️ Practice Problems

Problem 1: Triangle Angles

In triangle ABC, ∠A = 50° and ∠B = 70°. Find ∠C.

Answer: ∠C = 60°

Solution:

∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

50° + 70° + ∠C = 180°

120° + ∠C = 180°

∠C = 180° - 120° = 60°

Problem 2: Pythagorean Theorem

In a right triangle, if the legs are 6 and 8, find the hypotenuse.

Answer: Hypotenuse = 10

Solution:

a² + b² = c²

6² + 8² = c²

36 + 64 = c²

100 = c²

c = √100 = 10

Problem 3: Congruent Triangles

If triangle ABC ≅ triangle DEF and AB = 5, BC = 7, AC = 6, find the sides of triangle DEF.

Answer: DE = 5, EF = 7, DF = 6

Solution: Since the triangles are congruent, corresponding sides are equal.

DE = AB = 5

EF = BC = 7

DF = AC = 6
