üìñ 1. Linear Equations

Linear equations have variables with power 1 and form straight lines when graphed.

üìê Standard Form: ax + b = 0

Example: 2x + 3 = 7

Solution:

  1. Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x = 4
  2. Divide both sides by 2: x = 2

üîó 3. Systems of Linear Equations

Systems of equations have multiple equations with the same variables.

üìã Example: Solving by Substitution

Solve the system:

y = 2x + 1

3x + y = 10

Solution:

3x + (2x + 1) = 10

3x + 2x + 1 = 10

5x + 1 = 10

5x = 9

x = 1.8

y = 2(1.8) + 1 = 4.6

üìä Example: Solving by Elimination

Solve the system:

2x + 3y = 8

4x - y = 6

Solution:

Multiply second equation by 3:

2x + 3y = 8

12x - 3y = 18

Add equations: 14x = 26

x = 26/14 = 13/7

Substitute to find y
